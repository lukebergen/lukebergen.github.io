Question to the Divination Thing

If at will teleportation between sides is possible, what would my next best step be in figuring out how to do that?

A slender figure, wrapped in furs, leads a mass of people through a snowy expanse. The figure stops and you see the hint of a smirk from a face hidden in the hood of a cloak. Arms outstretched and raised you see reality bend and warp to their will as massive stone and ice edifices flow gracefully from the ground. The figure turns to the masses, “You try, now.” As a community forms, celebration erupts. The Children will create their home here with the guidance of their father. You see six figures, standing around a stone table deep in a wooded grove. You catch only passing details of each but their voices fill the clearing. “She’s gone too far.” “We must stop her.” “But the people believe in her, she’s the strongest of us.” A scoff. “You know what I mean. If the world’s belief was enough to shape her, isn’t her place in that world as just as our own?” A flash as time passes. Five figures stand huddled together as the sixth walks away, tears in her eyes, wanting no part in what’s to come. A place of learning and study where students walk the halls in scholarly robes before changing into homespun trousers and shirts. They line up, two by two, before a massive archway carved with dragons in flight. A professor sets a wooden case on a desk, unlatches and opens it up to reveal a series of metallic rods, each six inches long and made of a bright golden bronze metal. Even here, in this vision, you can sense the vibrations of each rod as it hums in harmony with the world around it. Each student walks up to take one, slipping it in their pocket, before stepping through to emerge in the same, but different place. Here to attend class of a different sort.
