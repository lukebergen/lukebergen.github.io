Alphabet

root > language > alphabet

Truelang uses the same alphabet as English except for Q and X because those are just dipthongs of kw and ks respectively.

Pronunciation is a bit different though as a lot of the dictionary derives from lojban as a base starting place and also to avoid phonastic ambiguity (English c vs k).

  • a: F*a*ther
  • b: *B*oy
  • c: *Sh*eet
  • d: *D*og
  • e: G*e*t
  • f: *F*ill
  • g: *G*et
  • h: *H*ive
  • i: M*ee*t
  • j: *J*am
  • k: *K*ite
  • l: *L*emon
  • m: *M*usic
  • n: *N*ight
  • o: *O*pen
  • p: *P*at
  • r: *R*at
  • s: *S*at
  • t: *T*ime
  • u: S**oo*n
  • v: *V*iolin
  • w: *W*in
  • y: F*u*n
  • z: *Z*ebra

TL;DR: English phonemes with a couple differences:

  • Q and X are gone.
  • "c" says "sh" instead of duplicating "k"
  • vowels do not pull double duty, they have exactly one pronunciation
